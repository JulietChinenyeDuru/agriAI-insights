The problem

An information gap, not an effort gap.

Nigerian agriculture employs over 36 million smallholder farmers — roughly 70% of the national labour force — yet operates without the satellite, soil-sensor, and ML-driven yield forecasts taken for granted elsewhere. AgriAI exists to change that.

What's inside

Built for the realities of rural deployment.

Every design decision is grounded in field constraints — patchy connectivity, low-end Android devices, multilingual users, and locally distinct agronomic conditions across six geopolitical zones.

🧠

Interpretable AI engine

A multivariate factor model — crop baseline × regional multiplier × soil × rainfall × fertilizer — with a confidence score. No black box.

🗺

Six-zone yield index

North-West, North-East, North-Central, South-West, South-East, South-South — each with its own rainfall norm and yield multiplier.

📶

Offline-first mobile UX

AsyncStorage caches predictions on device so the app remains useful when the cellular signal drops.

⚡

Async FastAPI backend

Sub-millisecond JSON serialisation, type-safe Pydantic v2 validation, auto-generated OpenAPI docs.

☁️

AWS-ready architecture

Container-first design that targets ECS Fargate + RDS PostgreSQL + CloudFront in eu-west-2.

🔓

Open source, MIT

Fork it, extend it, self-host it. Built explicitly to be a citable reference for African agritech research.

REST API

Seven endpoints, one well-documented surface.

The backend ships with auto-generated Swagger UI at /docs and Redoc at /redoc. All endpoints return JSON; all input is validated by Pydantic v2.

Privacy Policy

What AgriAI does — and does not — collect.

AgriAI is built around the principle that a farmer's data belongs to the farmer. This policy applies to the AgriAI mobile application and the AgriAI backend API.

Effective date: 19 May 2026 · Maintainer: Juliet Chinenye Duru

1. Information we collect

  • Farm data you submit: state, crop, farm size in hectares, and optionally rainfall, fertilizer use, and soil quality.
  • Prediction outputs: generated by our model from the inputs above.
  • Anonymous technical logs: request timestamps and endpoint names for the purpose of reliability monitoring.

2. Information we do not collect

  • We do not collect your name, phone number, email address, or any other personal identifier.
  • We do not collect or store GPS coordinates.
  • We do not use third-party advertising trackers or analytics SDKs.
  • We do not sell, rent, or share farm data with brokers or marketers.

3. Where your data lives

  • On your device: saved predictions are stored locally via AsyncStorage. Clearing the app's storage deletes them irreversibly.
  • On the server: the most recent 500 anonymised predictions may be retained in memory for the rolling /history endpoint. No personally identifying fields are stored.

4. Your rights

  • You may clear the on-device history at any time from the History screen.
  • You may operate AgriAI entirely offline; no data leaves your device unless you submit a prediction request.
  • You may self-host the backend — the source code is open under the MIT licence — to retain full control of any data you generate.

5. Children's privacy

AgriAI is intended for use by adults engaged in agricultural decision-making. It is not directed at children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect data from them.

6. Changes to this policy

Material changes will be published on this page with a new effective date and announced in the project's GitHub repository release notes.
